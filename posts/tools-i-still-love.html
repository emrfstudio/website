Tools I still love in 2024

A curated list of tools—both analog and digital—that have earned their stay in my setup. These aren't shiny new experiments; they're the ones that quietly deliver.

New tools are fun, but staying loyal to a few trusted companions has made my workflow calmer and far more reliable. Here's what I'm still using after years of iterating.

Analog allies

Baron Fig Confidant notebook. Durable enough to travel, thick paper that loves fountain pens, and a format that encourages big thinking. Each quarter gets its own notebook.

Uni-ball Signo DX 0.38. Writes like a dream, dries quickly, and keeps my handwriting crisp. I stock up once a year and never worry about running out.

Digital staples

Obsidian. My second brain lives here. I rely on daily notes, a simple tagging system, and spaced repetition plug-ins to resurface ideas on schedule.

Figma. For everything from wireframes to mood boards, Figma is my playground. Real-time collaboration makes it an easy choice.

Alfred. A keyboard-driven command center that replaces a handful of apps. Custom workflows help me launch projects, snippets, and timers without touching the mouse.

Rituals matter more than tools

The best tools fade into the background. They support the habits that matter and remove friction from doing the work. Whenever I'm tempted to chase something new, I revisit this list and ask whether a ritual needs improvement before the tool does.
