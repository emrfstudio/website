Designing a life you love

A reflection on aligning daily routines with long-term dreams, plus the questions I revisit every season to make sure my calendar reflects my priorities.

The first step in designing a life you love is to decide what belongs in it. Not in the abstract, but in vivid, calendar-level detail. My template starts with three pillars: creative output, deep relationships, and consistent learning. Everything I say yes to should ladder up to one of these three.

Every quarter I schedule a personal review day. I open my journal, map the commitments that shaped the previous months, and compare them to the intentions I set. The goal isn't to judge myself for mismatches—it is to observe the gap with curiosity.

Questions that guide my review

  • What energized me most in the past season? What drained me?
  • Where did I ship meaningful work? Where did I stall?
  • Which relationships did I invest in? Who do I want to spend more time with?
  • What am I learning that excites me enough to keep going deeper?

These reflections feed into a planning session where I design experiments. Sometimes they're small: batching meetings into one afternoon. Other times they're bigger: blocking a weekly "studio day" with no notifications and a single focus. The key is to treat each change as a hypothesis instead of a permanent switch.

Build from the calendar up

To keep myself honest, I translate every priority onto the calendar. Creative work becomes focus blocks twice a week. Relationship building becomes a recurring dinner with friends. Learning shows up as a Saturday morning study club with my favorite coffee and a stack of books.

Designing a life you love is never finished. It is a living document that adapts as you do. The win is not achieving a perfect schedule—it's staying in conversation with what matters most.
